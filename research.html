Research Interests

I’m interested in building AI systems that are powerful, interpretable, and grounded in real-world applications.

Interpretable & Explicit Neural Architectures

I’m working towards models that provide explicit mathematical representations of the relationships they learn, rather than acting as pure black boxes. This includes polynomial-style networks and architectures where the learned mapping between inputs and outputs can be expressed and reasoned about directly.

Interpretable AI Polynomial Networks Symbolic-style Reasoning
Multimodal AI (Vision + Language + Time-Series)

My internships and projects have pushed me towards multimodal settings: Visual Question Answering, stock market prediction from heterogeneous signals, and interfaces that combine 3D medical imagery with textual/structured data. I’m interested in how transformers and attention mechanisms can fuse modalities while still retaining a level of interpretability.

VQA Transformers Multimodal Fusion
AI for Physical Systems & Manufacturing

Through my work at the Center for Additive Manufacturing, I’ve seen how AI can help compress complex physical interactions into usable models: predicting strength, detecting printing defects, and optimizing process parameters. I’m interested in extending these ideas to other engineering domains with rich sensor data.

Additive Manufacturing Time-series + Vision
Long-term Goals

My long-term goal is to pursue graduate studies and contribute to core research in machine learning and optimization, especially in directions that make AI more explainable and trustworthy. I want to work at the intersection of theory, architecture design, and practical deployment.
